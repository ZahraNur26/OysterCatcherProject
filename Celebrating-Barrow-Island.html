Celebrating Barrow Island

Artistic or media piece which highlights the integration of Aboriginal Science & Culture with the uniques of Barrow Island's Flora and Fauna

Barrow Island Inside a Jar

This art piece is a reflection of not only our business unit animal, but of Barrow Island and itâ€™s ecosystem. The layers of sand represent the different layers of rock that lie beneath Barrow Island. The fossils which have been preserved in the layers of rock are represented by a variety of colored rocks.

The blue sand represents the Indian ocean which surrounds Barrow Island. Using 3D printing, we have created Barrow Island and an Oyster Catcher bird. The Oyster Catcher is a bird native to Barrow Island and is the animal which our Business Unit is named after.

The Aboriginal dot painting around the jar represents the sky using blue and white dots as well as an orange and yellow sun. The lid of the jar represents the galaxy in which we live in, showing the stars of beyond. These elements combine to create a glimpse of Barrow Island, showing the foundations of our business unit.
